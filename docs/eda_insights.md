# EDA Insights

Generated by `notebooks/01_eda.ipynb` using non-cancelled orders only.

## Business Insights

1. Total demand peaks in 2024-03 and is lowest in 2024-02. Demand planning should review PET Chips coverage before the peak month instead of reacting after orders arrive.
2. Carpet Polyester Yarn is the highest-demand product with 5,797.2 tons. This product should receive the first weekly forecast review.
3. The top customer is Atlas Carpet Industries with 3,766.4 tons. Losing or delaying this account would create a visible demand planning gap.
4. The top five customers represent 76.1% of total demand. Account-level sales notes from these customers are especially important early demand signals.
5. High Tenacity Polyester Yarn has the highest demand volatility with a coefficient of variation of 0.55. This product needs closer safety-stock and production review.
6. PET price index and weekly demand show a weak positive relationship (correlation 0.02). Price changes should be monitored as a demand-timing signal, not treated as the only demand driver.
7. USD rate and weekly demand show a weak negative relationship (correlation -0.02). Export-sensitive customers may need separate review when exchange-rate pressure changes.
8. Export-related customers represent 68.8% of total demand. Splitting export and non-export demand helps planners avoid hiding export risk inside one total number.

- The next useful step is a simple weekly baseline forecast by product, then a comparison with sales-note signals.
